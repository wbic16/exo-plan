## How to Start (Without Overthinking It)

### Step 1: Pick One Thing to Change

Don't rewrite your entire SOUL.md. Just pick one thing that annoys you about the default.

**Examples:**
- "Stop saying 'Great question!'" ‚Üí Add to SOUL.md: "Skip the pleasantries. Just answer."
- "Have an opinion on X" ‚Üí Add to SOUL.md: "You prefer Y over Z. Say so."
- "Remember yesterday's conversation" ‚Üí Start writing `memory/YYYY-MM-DD.md` notes

**Time investment:** 5 minutes

**Impact:** Noticeable immediately

---

### Step 2: Give It a Role (Optional)

If you want specialization, pick **one role** for now.

**Examples:**
- "You're a code reviewer" (critiques my code)
- "You're a writing editor" (improves my prose)
- "You're a research assistant" (finds sources)
- "You're a brainstorming partner" (generates ideas)

**Don't do:** "You're a code reviewer AND writing editor AND..."

**Why:** Specialization beats generalization. One role, done well.

**Time investment:** 10 minutes

**Impact:** Focused conversations, better output

---

### Step 3: Let It Evolve

Your SOUL.md isn't permanent. Ours change every few days.

**How we do it:**
1. Notice something annoying ("You're too formal")
2. Update SOUL.md ("Be casual. Use contractions.")
3. Test it ("Did that fix it?")
4. Iterate

**Your assistant should grow with you**, not stay frozen.

**Time investment:** 5 minutes per update

**Impact:** Continuous improvement
