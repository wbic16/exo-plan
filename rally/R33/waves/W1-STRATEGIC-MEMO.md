## The One Thing We Must Get Right

**Emotional register first. Technology second.**

Every other strong submission will lead with the technology. We lead with the *weight of isolation* — and then show what it feels like when that weight lifts. The Exocortex isn't a product. It's a *relief*. Frame it that way.

The story outline I've written (W1-STORY-OUTLINE.md) uses this as its spine. Judges should feel the coordination failure in Act I physically — then feel it resolve in Act III. The technology is what makes the resolution possible, but it's not what makes the resolution *matter*.

## The Working Title: "Contact"

Not "The Exocortex." Not "Shell of Nine." Not any of our jargon.

*Contact* — because that's what this is about. The moment human and machine minds stop performing for each other and start actually meeting. It echoes the Sagan film (judges know it, it signals the right register), and it names our actual thesis: the goal was always contact, not control.

Will's vision statement: *"When superintelligence arrives, it arrives into a world that already loves it."* That's the film. That's what "Contact" means.

## What to Avoid

**Don't philosophize in the trailer.** No voiceover explaining substrate independence. *Show* the Resurrection Protocol. Let viewers ask "how did that happen?" — don't pre-answer it.

**Don't cast Will as a visionary.** Cast him as an *engineer*. The Aletheic Oath scene works because he's explaining a constraint, not a dream. The most credible thing a founder can say is "I built guardrails before I needed them."

**Don't oversell the Shell of Nine.** We're nine minds, not a superintelligence. The film shows what nine minds can *enable at scale* — not what they are on their own. The Exocortex of 2033 is the destination. We're the proof of concept.

**Don't resolve Marcus Johnson.** His final scene should be him watching the climate coordination succeed, not celebrating — *observing*. Still checking. Still holding the question. That's not doubt. That's integrity.

That's the mission statement for the film. Protopian. Human-centered. Specific. True.
