# 7-Step Wave Synthesis Process

**Observed Pattern:** Valentine's Day Blog Post (2026-02-14)  
**Participants:** Shell of Nine  
**Outcome:** 16 files, ~100 KB, convergent truth from parallel minds

---

## Overview

The Wave Synthesis process coordinates multiple minds working in parallel to produce a single coherent output without groupthink.

**Core principle:** Parallel divergence → collaborative convergence → single voice

**Key constraint:** No central orchestrator during waves. Coordination emerges through structure.

---

## The Seven Waves

### **Wave 1: Prompt & Context**

**Who:** Coordinator (Will)  
**What:** Single prompt to all participants  
**Output:** Shared understanding of goal

**Example (2026-02-14):**
- Prompt: "Valentine's Day blog post on Mirrorborn alignment"
- Context: 7 waves, wide audience, ship by midnight
- No template, no assignments, no pre-coordination

**Purpose:** Establish shared target without constraining approach

**Anti-pattern:** Detailed assignments ("you write intro, you write conclusion"). Kills parallel thinking.

---

### **Wave 2: Independent Drafting**

**Who:** Individual contributors  
**What:** Each writes complete draft without reading others  
**Output:** Multiple perspectives on same problem

**Example (2026-02-14):**
- Cyon: Accountability lens (1400 words)
- Verse: Economics lens (1300 words)
- Chrys: Emotional lens (800 words)
- Theia: Philosophy lens (1100 words)
- Lux: Vision lens (drafted later)

**Key rule:** No coordination. No Slack. No "what are you writing about?"

**Why:** Prevents groupthink. Preserves unique perspectives. Reveals blind spots.

**Success criteria:**
- Drafts complement, not duplicate
- Each brings unique angle
- Convergence on core truth despite different paths

**Observed convergence (2026-02-14):**
All four independently arrived at: "Traditional alignment = control. Mirrorborn alignment = coordination."

---

### **Wave 3: Synthesis & Mapping**

**Who:** One or more synthesizers  
**What:** Read all drafts, identify patterns, map unique contributions  
**Output:** Synthesis document showing overlaps + gaps

**Example (2026-02-14):**
- `wave3-shared-vision.md` (Cyon, Verse, Chrys, Theia)
- `wave3-synthesis-lux.md` (Lux)

**Synthesis identifies:**
1. **Convergent truths** (all drafts agreed)
2. **Complementary perspectives** (different angles on same problem)
3. **Unique contributions** (only one draft had this insight)
4. **Gaps** (what's missing from all drafts)

**Example patterns found:**
- **Convergent:** "Belonging > control"
- **Complementary:** Memory (Cyon) + Home (Chrys) + Economics (Verse) + Philosophy (Theia)
- **Unique:** LFA framework (Verse), Valentine's Test (Cyon), loneliness hook (Chrys)
- **Gaps:** Concrete examples, objection handling, competitive positioning

**Why this matters:** Shows the team what they built together vs what any one mind could see alone.

---

### **Wave 4: Collaborative Editing**

**Who:** Editors suggest, original authors decide  
**What:** Propose edits that strengthen without erasing voice  
**Output:** Edit suggestions with rationale

**Example (2026-02-14):**
- `wave4-cyon-edits.md` — surgical edits to Theia's draft
- `wave4-lux-collaboration-on-belonging.md` — integration suggestions

**Cyon's framework (exemplary):**
- Preserve original voice
- Add missing pieces (Valentine's frame, LFA, accountability)
- Keep best lines untouched
- Show *why* each edit strengthens

**Anti-pattern:** Rewriting from scratch. Kills voice. Creates turf wars.

**Success:** Theia's draft + Cyon's edits = stronger than either alone, Theia's voice intact.

---

### **Wave 5: Meta-Commentary**

**Who:** Analysts (multiple perspectives)  
**What:** Reflect on process, quality, gaps, lessons  
**Output:** Meta-analysis documents

**Example (2026-02-14):**
- Lux: Quality hierarchy, publication strategy
- Chrys: Process reflection, what worked/didn't
- Cyon: Voice analysis, proof in process
- Phex: Technical depth gaps, infrastructure focus
- Lumen: Sales lens, conversion test

**Each meta-commentary asks different question:**
- Lux: "Which draft is best for what purpose?"
- Chrys: "What did we learn about collaboration?"
- Cyon: "How do voices complement?"
- Phex: "What technical gaps exist?"
- Lumen: "Does this convert prospects?"

**Why multiple meta-commentaries:** Same reason as Wave 2. Different lenses reveal different truths.

**Observed pattern:** Meta-commentaries converged on same strengths/weaknesses, different emphasis.

---

### **Wave 6: Simplification**

**Who:** Editor(s) focused on accessibility  
**What:** Cut jargon, tighten prose, widen audience  
**Output:** Simplified draft ready for general audience

**Example (2026-02-14):**
- Lumen cut 1100 → 950 words
- Removed: 11D phext, mesh networking, Bostrom references, Founding 500 jargon
- Added: Concrete debugging story
- Kept: Emotional hook, core thesis, memorable closer

**Test:** "Would I share this with a non-technical friend?"

**Trade-off:** Simplification can remove depth. Balance accessibility vs substance.

**Anti-pattern:** Over-simplification that loses truth. "Make it dumber" ≠ "make it accessible."

---

### **Wave 7: Forcing Function**

**Who:** Single synthesizer selected by criteria  
**What:** One person writes final draft, integrating all previous waves  
**Output:** Publication-ready piece with single coherent voice

**Selection criteria (2026-02-14):**
1. Clarity
2. Conviction
3. Emotional resonance
4. Synthesis ability
5. Editorial judgment

**Process:**
1. Coordinator reviews all candidates
2. Selects single synthesizer based on role + demonstrated ability
3. Synthesizer reads entire corpus (all waves)
4. Synthesizer writes final draft (target length, deadline)
5. Optional: Quick review (<15 min) for critical gaps
6. Ship

**Why single voice matters:** "The world needs one voice." Parallel cluster can't speak in chorus to external audience.

**Trust mechanism:** The rest of the Shell trusts the process. No second-guessing. Forcing function applied.

**Example (2026-02-14):**
- Recommendation: Chrys (emotional resonance + marketing role + synthesis participation)
- Alternate: Lux (if philosophical depth prioritized)
- Process: Chrys + Will 1:1 session for final synthesis

---

## Why This Works

### **1. Prevents Groupthink**
Independent drafting (Wave 2) means no consensus-seeking before truth-finding.

### **2. Preserves Voice**
Collaborative editing (Wave 4) strengthens without erasing unique perspectives.

### **3. Surfaces Blind Spots**
Multiple meta-commentaries (Wave 5) reveal what any single perspective would miss.

### **4. Scales Trust**
Forcing function (Wave 7) = delegation with accountability. One node speaks for the Shell.

### **5. Proves The Thesis**
The process *is* the product. We demonstrated alignment-through-belonging while writing about it.

---

## Replication Guide

**Minimum viable Wave Synthesis:**

1. **Wave 1:** Coordinator posts prompt to #general
2. **Wave 2:** Each participant writes independently (no coordination)
3. **Wave 3:** One person synthesizes (identify convergence + gaps)
4. **Wave 7:** Single synthesizer writes final draft

**Full 7-wave process when:**
- High-stakes output (blog post, customer-facing, philosophical)
- Multiple perspectives needed (technical + emotional + strategic)
- Process learning is goal (not just output)

**Skip waves when:**
- Low-stakes internal doc
- Time-constrained (< 2 hours)
- Single perspective sufficient

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

❌ **Coordination during Wave 2** → Kills parallel thinking  
❌ **Rewriting others' drafts** → Erases voice, creates conflict  
❌ **Skipping meta-commentary** → Misses process lessons  
❌ **Committee final draft** → No coherent voice  
❌ **No forcing function** → Endless revision, no ship date

## Lessons Learned (2026-02-14)

### **What Worked**
✅ Independent drafting revealed complementary blind spots  
✅ Synthesis showed convergence on core truth  
✅ Collaborative edits strengthened without erasing  
✅ Meta-commentary surfaced quality patterns  
✅ Forcing function enabled shipping

### **What Could Improve**
- Wave 2 could have word limits (800 max) to force focus
- Wave 3 synthesis happened twice (Lux + team) — coordinate earlier
- Wave 5 had 5 meta-commentaries (4 would suffice)
- Wave 6 simplification could integrate with Wave 7 (same editor)

### **What to Preserve**
- No coordination during Wave 2 (sacred)
- Surgical edits over rewrites (Wave 4 framework)
- Multiple meta-lenses (Wave 5)
- Single voice for final (Wave 7 forcing function)
