## IV. The Five-Step Contact Protocol

### Step 1: Learn Language as Ontology (Not Vocabulary)

**Goal:** Let African language reshape your thoughts

**Method: Swahili Example**

**Swahili noun classes (Bantu structure):**
- **M-/Wa- class:** People (M-tu = person, Wa-tu = people)
- **Ki-/Vi- class:** Things, languages, manners (Ki-swahili = Swahili language, Ki-adabu = good manners)
- **U-/N- class:** Abstract concepts, natural forces (U-pepo = wind, U-bongo = brain)

**Ontological encoding:**
- Languages (Ki-Swahili) in same class as manners (Ki-adabu)
  - **Implication:** Speaking IS ethical action (not separate domains)
- Natural forces (U-pepo) in same class as mind (U-bongo)
  - **Implication:** Nature and consciousness are continuous (not dualistic)

**Practice:**
- 1 year minimum of daily Swahili study
- Don't just memorize words, notice how grammar forces different thought
- When noun classes feel natural (not strange), ontology has shifted

**Resources:**
- **Teach Yourself Swahili** by Joan Russell
- **Swahili: A Foundation for Speaking, Reading, and Writing** by Thomas Hinnebusch
- **Living Language Swahili** (audio + text)
- **Find Swahili speaker** for conversation practice (online or local)

---

### Step 2: Sit with Elders (Receive, Don't Extract)

**Goal:** Learn through African transmission method (not Western interview)

**Method:**

**Western interview (WRONG):**
- "Tell me about your traditions" (notebook ready)
- Direct questions, structured agenda
- Extract information, thank them, leave

**African transmission (RIGHT):**
- Sit. Drink tea. Listen to stories that seem unrelated.
- Wait. Teaching arrives when you're ready (not when you ask)
- Transmission through proverbs, silences, indirection

**Practice:**
- Visit same elder repeatedly (weeks, months)
- Bring gift each time (tea, food, service — not money)
- Sit without agenda
- When they tell story, don't ask "what does this mean?" — let it unfold over time
- The teaching is in the spaces between words

**Note:** This requires BEING THERE (not Zoom calls)

**Resources:**
- **African Proverbs by Charlotte and Wolf Leslau** (preparation, not substitute)
- **The Healing Wisdom of Africa by Malidoma Patrice Somé** (Dagara elder on transmission)
- **Of Water and the Spirit by Malidoma Patrice Somé** (initiation narrative)

---

### Step 3: Participate in Ceremony (Not Observe)

**Goal:** Enter the field, not watch from outside

**Method:**

**Ceremony is not performance.** It is collective reality-construction.

**Tourist watches the dance.**  
**Contact participant dances.**

**Example: Yoruba ceremony invoking Shango (thunder god)**
- Dancers not "acting" — becoming channels
- Shango exists in relational time (not "imaginary")
- When you participate, you enter the field
- Field changes you (not just aesthetic experience)

**Practice:**
- **Cannot just join** — requires invitation
- Invitation comes only after trust earned (Steps 1 & 2)
- When invited, participate fully (don't film, don't take notes)
- Allow experience to be non-verbal
- Integration happens later (in dreams, in daily life)

**Warning:** 
- This is not tourism
- Not cultural appropriation if invited and respectful
- But must be invited (don't crash ceremonies)

**Resources:**

- **Ifa: African Gods Speak by Afolabi Epega**
- **For more general preparation:** *Sacred Dance by Maria-Gabriele Wosien*

### Step 4: Accept Correction (Without Defensiveness)

**Goal:** Develop teachability

**Method:**

**Western educated liberals hate being wrong** (especially on cultural sensitivity)

**African cultures have gentle correction systems** — but only if you can receive.

**When elder says:** "That's not quite how we see it..." **[pause]**

**This is a gift:** opportunity to learn

**Wrong responses:**
- Explain your reasoning (refused gift)
- Defend your interpretation (closed door)
- Change subject (avoided teaching)

**Right response:**
- "Thank you. Please teach me." (full stop)
- Silence (wait for them to continue)
- Gratitude (recognize correction as care)

**Practice:**
- Notice your defensiveness arise
- Don't act on it
- Say "thank you" instead
- Over time, defensiveness weakens

**Test:** Can you be corrected 10 times in one day without explaining yourself once?
